## 1. Shiva–Shakti / Origin of Tantra / Agama–Nigama

- Shaiva & Shakta Agama texts are framed as a **dialogue between Shiva and Shakti**.
  - When **Shiva teaches Shakti** → the text is called **Āgama**.
  - When **Shakti teaches Shiva** → the text is called **Nigama** (also loosely "Tantra").
- Agamas are, in the limited sense, tantric texts — principles + practice — presented as
  Shiva teaching Parvati. In Shaivism, Agamas rank alongside (or above) the Vedas and are
  the foundation of Shaiva, Tantra and Shakta traditions "since the earliest times."
- Traditional count often cited: **5 Nigamas and 64 Agamas** (numbers vary by school).
- Classification of Agamas by principal deity: **Shaiva, Vaishnava, Shakta (Tantra)**.
  - Shaiva: 28 Shaiva Siddhanta Agamas + 64 (Pashupata / Kashmiri Trika).
  - Shakta tradition: **Kaula, Mishra, Samaya** agamas.
- Tantra ≈ Shakta Agamas — worships the **Divine Mother (Shakti)** as supreme.

## 2. Kali Kula / Krama / Kaula / Kashmir Shaivism

- "Kashmir Shaivism" = 19th-c. umbrella term for non-dual Shaiva-Shakta tantric literature
  from Kashmir; **principally Trika and Krama**, both sub-schools of the **Kaula** tradition.
- **Krama** = the system that keeps the *cyclic* (kramic) structure of the path to reality;
  it is the **cult of Kālī**. Abhinavagupta drew heavily on the Kālī-based Krama of the Kulamārga.
- **Kula / Kaula** from *kula* = "family/group" → spiritual family bound by esoteric knowledge.
  Closely tied to Shaktism (Goddess worship). Rituals center on Kali, Tripura Sundari, Durga.
- Kaula emphasizes worship of **Shakti** (feminine energy) + **Shiva** (supreme consciousness),
  and uses ritual, the body, and sensory experience as a *path to liberation* (rather than
  renouncing them). Contrast with the ascetic/renunciate model.

Note for guide: "Kali Kula" (the Kālī-family lineage of the North, associated with Krama)
is often contrasted with "Shri Kula" (the Tripura-Sundari/Shri Vidya family of the South).

## 3. Shri Vidya / Sri Chakra / Lalita Tripura Sundari / Panchadasi

- **Panchadasi mantra** = 15-syllable mantra of the Shri Vidya tradition, to Tripura Sundari
  (Lalita / Rajarajeshwari).
  - Common form: **ka e i la hrīṃ / ha sa ka ha la hrīṃ / sa ka la hrīṃ**.
  - Three **kutas** (groups):
    1. **Vagbhava Kuta** (ka e i la hrim) — knowledge / speech.
    2. **Kamaraja Kuta** (ha sa ka ha la hrim) — desire / creation.
    3. **Shakti Kuta** (sa ka la hrim) — action / fulfilment.
  - The 16-syllable form is **Shodashi** (adds a secret syllable).
- **Tripura Sundari** = "Beautiful One of the Three Worlds" — dominion over physical, astral,
  causal realms. Supreme Goddess of Shri Vidya.
- **Sri Chakra / Sri Yantra** = geometric mandala = the universe AND the Goddess's body;
  macrocosm = microcosm (also mapped onto the human body). Goddess resides in the central
  **bindu**. Worshipped via **Navavarana puja** (nine enclosures).
- Kept secret; taught only under a qualified guru. Gateway to self-realization / bliss / union.
- Shri Vidya is unique among the Mahavidyas: grants **both bhoga and moksha** through one mantra
  because it inherently unites Shiva and Shakti.

## 4. Pancha Kosha (Five Sheaths) — Taittiriya Upanishad 2.1–5

From outermost → innermost:
1. **Annamaya kosha** — "food/physical" body (gross).
2. **Pranamaya kosha** — energy/breath body.
3. **Manomaya kosha** — mind/emotion body.
4. **Vijnanamaya kosha** — wisdom/intellect body.
5. **Anandamaya kosha** — bliss body (causal).

Mapping to the three bodies:
- **Sthula sharira** (gross body) = Annamaya.
- **Sukshma sharira** (subtle body) = Pranamaya + Manomaya + Vijnanamaya.
- **Karana sharira** (causal body) = Anandamaya.

The koshas are like nested layers (onion / Russian dolls) surrounding the innermost
**Atman** — unchanging awareness.

## 6. Bija (seed) Mantras

- **Bija** = "seed"; single-syllable sound-seeds that activate/purify chakra energies.
- Chakra bijas: LAM, VAM, RAM, YAM, HAM, OM (Ajna). (Crown often silent.)
- Effects (popular attributions): LAM grounding/security; VAM creativity/emotion;
  RAM personal power; YAM love/compassion; HAM communication/truth.
- Goddess/deity bijas:
  - **OM / AUM** — pranava, the primordial sound (A-U-M + silence).
  - **HRĪṂ** — "Maya bija" / Bhuvaneshwari; dissolves illusion of good & evil.
  - **SHRĪṂ** — Lakshmi; beauty, abundance, wealth, wellbeing.
  - **KLĪṂ** — "Kama bija"; attraction, desire, Kamadeva/Krishna/Kali.
  - **KRĪṂ** — Kali bija; power, transformation, dissolution.
  - **AIṂ** — Saraswati bija; speech, knowledge, arts.
  - **DUṂ** — Durga bija; protection.
  - **GAṂ** — Ganapati bija; removing obstacles.

## 7. Dasha Mahavidya (Ten Wisdom Goddesses)

Sequence: **Kali, Tara, Tripura Sundari, Bhuvaneshvari, Bhairavi, Chhinnamasta,
Dhumavati, Bagalamukhi, Matangi, Kamalatmika.**

Meanings (popular):
- Kali — fierce transformation, courage, time/death.
- Tara — compassionate guide through chaos.
- Tripura Sundari — radiance, beauty, abundance, harmony.
- Bhuvaneshvari — vastness, space, expansion of vision.
- Bhairavi — discipline, tapas, fierce power.
- Chhinnamasta — ego surrender, kundalini, self-sacrifice.
- Dhumavati — wisdom in endings, loss, the void.
- Bagalamukhi — stillness, mastery over negativity/enemies.
- Matangi — inner voice, raw authenticity, arts, the "outcaste" goddess.
- Kamalatmika — prosperity, grace, sweetness (Lakshmi-like).

Some split them into **benign** (Tara, Tripura Sundari, Kamala, Bhuvaneshvari, Matangi) and
**fierce** (Kali, Dhumavati, Bhairavi, Chhinnamasta, Bagalamukhi).

## 8. Patanjali's Ashtanga Yoga & Kundalini

- **Ashtanga** = 8 limbs (Yoga Sutras): Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara,
  Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi.
- Not a strict ladder — mutually reinforcing; culminate in **Samadhi** (absorption/union).
- Patanjali's system (Raja Yoga) does **not** itself center kundalini/chakras — that vocabulary
  belongs to Tantra & Hatha Yoga. But the frameworks are complementary: Ashtanga provides the
  ethical/mental discipline; Tantra/Hatha adds the subtle-body (nadi/chakra/kundalini) map.

Sources:
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashtanga_(eight_limbs_of_yoga)
- https://www.shvasa.com/yoga-blog/eight-limbs-of-yoga-ashtanga-yoga

## 9. Kundalini / Nadis / Granthis

- **Kundalini shakti** = coiled energy at base of spine (Muladhara); rises through
  **Sushumna** (central channel) to **Sahasrara** → union with Shiva.
- **Ida** (left, lunar, cooling, feminine) & **Pingala** (right, solar, heating, masculine)
  spiral around Sushumna. When balanced, Sushumna opens and kundalini can rise.
- **Three granthis** (psychic knots) that must be pierced:
  - **Brahma granthi** — at Muladhara; bondage to desire/physical/security.
  - **Vishnu granthi** — at Manipura/Anahata; bondage to action/emotion.
  - **Rudra granthi** — at Ajna; bondage to thought/attachment to powers.
- Culmination: Shiva–Shakti union at Sahasrara → individual merges with divine consciousness.

## 10. Guru–Shishya / Diksha

- **Guru–shishya parampara** = lineage/succession of teacher→disciple; ensures continuity of
  **sampradaya** (tradition) and transmission of dharma & subtle knowledge.
- **Diksha** = formal initiation; guru accepts disciple and responsibility for their spiritual
  progress; mantra diksha often whispered in the right ear.
- Especially important in Tantra: potent/secret practices require personal transmission,
  correction, and protection. Based on genuineness of guru + devotion/obedience of disciple.

## 11. How to Start Sadhana

- Four stages (traditional): **Murti Seva** (image worship) → **Japa & Stuti** (chanting) →
  **Dhyana-Dharana** (meditation) → **Brahma Sadhana** (cosmic ideation).
- Beginners: start with **Nama Japa** (name-repetition). "In Kaliyuga, japa is the feasible
  sadhana." Do NOT begin with advanced Tantric kriya or forced kundalini rousing —
  "like a loaded firearm in a child's hands."
- Daily **puja**: worship Sun, Shiva, Shakti, Vishnu, Ganesha. **16 upacharas** (offerings):
  asana, svagata, padya, arghya, achamana, madhuparka, snana, vasana, abharana, gandha,
  pushpa, dhupa, dipa, naivedya, vandana...
- Begin with **Hatha yoga / asana** for steadiness; then focus (breath / sound / image).
- Preparatory discipline: **brahmacharya**, sattvic/havishya food, bathing, regularity, purity.

## 12. Goal of Tantra — Bhukti & Mukti

- Shakta Tantra claims to give **both** Enjoyment (**Bhukti/Bhoga**) in the world AND
  Liberation (**Mukti**) from all worlds — no dichotomy between them.
- "Bhoga becomes Yoga, vice becomes virtue, the enslaving world becomes the means to liberation."
- **Jivanmukti** = liberation *while living* — the jivanmukta is free even while embodied.
- Union of **Shiva & Shakti** (at Sahasrara / in the heart) = the realized goal.
- *Mahanirvana Tantra* = "Tantra of the Great Liberation."
